The darkage of Console Game.

From 1980 to 1995, the global console game sales went up and down. The future of the console game at that time was not clear.



Here comes Atari!

Atari was the only game-console at early 80s.

In 1981, the most famous game Pac-man lead to a peak of Atari game salse!

However, Atari did not control their game quality and allowed game developers to copy a game and rebrand it.

This finally resulted in Atari Shock, a video game crash happening at 1982 - 1984.

At the end, Atari bankrupted ...

Long live Nintendo!

After this shock, Nintendo took the chance and made a commerical success with three consoles NES, Super NES, and Game Body(GB) in the following 10 years.

To aviod the Atari Shock, Nintendo was applying a higher tax for games published on NES and SNES. In this way, game developers would put more time on how to imporve game quality.

During this time, the Donkey Franchise and Mario Franchise became the most popular game series.


However, the monoploy position and restrict rule left resistance among game developers ...

Nintendo is evil!

With the high-pressure policy Nintendo were using, more and more game companies started to challenge Nintendo's position.

Tens of game-consoles including Sega's gen and Sony's ps1 were in the market. While, none of them were able to take Nintendo down!

However, this open competition did stimulate the whole market ...

New Age

Years later ...

Sony and its PlayStation 1 took the market with almost 80% of total maker share !

This also lead to a rapid growth in the game market.

Big titles like NBA, FiFA, Street Fighter, and Metal Gear Solid were in the platform.

Sony V.S. Nintendo V.S MicroSoft

The console game market was all about Sony and Nintendo during this perioud.

Sony released PS2, MicroSoft released XBox(XB) and Nintendo released GBA/GC/N64 to consolidate their market shares.
